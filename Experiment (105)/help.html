About the experiment

The experiment shows a stationary Box constrained by strings on left and right.

Animation control

The top line has animation controls. There are two states of the experiment.

The setup stage

The initial state is setup stage. In this stage, you can see a control window at the right. You have access six sliders.

You can control the following:

  • weight1  : Controls the mass(in tonnes) of weight1(leftmost weight).
  • weight2  : Controls the mass(in tonnes) of weight2( 2nd from left )
  • weight3 : Controls the mass(in tonnes) of weight3( 3rd from left ).
  • weight4 : Controls the mass(in tonnes) of weight4( third from right )
  • weight5 : Controls the mass(in tonnes) of weight5( second from right ).
  • weight6 : Controls the mass(in tonnes) of weight6( first from right ).

In addition you will also see a digital clock which shows the time during the which the block moves.

after choosing the appropriate masses of weights,press start to see the result

Once you setup the experiment, you can enter the animation stage by clicking the start button

The animation stage

In the animation stage, the box will move around obeying the laws of physics.

The right hand panel now shows the values of the six experiment variables during animation.

  • weight1  : shows the mass(in tonnes) of weight1(leftmost weight) during aninmation.
  • weight2  : shows the mass(in tonnes) of weight2( 2nd from left ) during animation.
  • weight3 : shows the mass(in tonnes) of weight3( 3rd from left ) during animation.
  • weight4 : shows the mass(in tonnes) of weight4( third from right ) during animation
  • weight5 : shows the mass(in tonnes) of weight5( second from right ) during animation.
  • weight6 : shows the mass(in tonnes) of weight6( first from right ) during animation.

You can pause and resume the animation by using the pause/play nutton on the top line

You can slow down and speed up the animaion by using the speed control button.

You can reset the animation by pressing the reset button
